## 1. Plant at 8-12 Inch Spacing — The "Wait 5 Years" Approach Costs More in Weeding

At 24-inch spacing, ground covers take 3-4 years to fill in. During those years, you are weeding bare soil weekly. At 12-inch spacing, coverage in 1.5-2 seasons. At 8-inch spacing, coverage in 1-1.5 seasons. The extra plants cost $30-$60 more but save 50+ hours of weeding. Buy the plants.

## 2. Creeping Thyme Is the Best Stepable Ground Cover — and It Smells Amazing

Thymus serpyllum tolerates light foot traffic (1-2 walks/day), releases fragrance, and covers itself in pink-purple flowers in June-July. Zones 4-9. Full sun. 2-3 inches tall. The one downside: blooming thyme attracts bees — do not plant as a primary walkway near doors or patios.

## 3. Never Plant English Ivy, Vinca, or Winter Creeper

These three species are Category 1 invasives that escape cultivation, destroy forests, and cannot be removed once established. The alternatives: creeping phlox (sun), Allegheny spurge (native pachysandra, shade), or liriope (any condition). Every gardener who has ever planted English ivy has either regretted it or is still in denial.

## 4. Sheet Mulching Kills Grass Without Chemicals or Labor

Lay cardboard over grass (removing tape/staples), overlap edges 6 inches, wet thoroughly, cover with 3-4 inches of compost or mulch. The grass dies in 2-3 months. Plant ground covers directly through the mulch. The cardboard and grass decompose into soil. Zero herbicides, zero sod-cutting, zero tilling.

## 5. On Slopes, Plant in Staggered Rows — Not Straight Lines

A staggered diamond pattern intercepts water flowing downhill — each plant acts as a mini-dam. Straight rows channel water into erosion gullies. Cover bare soil between plants with jute or coir erosion-control blanket ($25 per 4'' × 50'' roll) that biodegrades over 2-3 years as plants fill in.

## 6. 110 Plants Cover 100 Square Feet at 12-Inch Spacing

Formula: (square feet) ÷ (spacing in feet)² × 1.1 (10% extra for losses). For a 200 sq ft area at 12-inch spacing: 200 ÷ 1 × 1.1 = 220 plants. Buy flats (36-72 plants per flat, $20-$40) rather than individual 4-inch pots ($3-$5 each). Flats are the bulk discount — 1/3 the price per plant.

## 7. Epimedium Is the Unkillable Dry-Shade Ground Cover

Under mature trees where nothing else grows (dry, shaded, root competition), epimedium survives and slowly spreads. It takes 2-3 years to establish and 5+ years to form a dense mat, but it requires zero maintenance once established. Delicate spring flowers, attractive foliage. Zones 4-9.

## 8. Creeping Jenny Spreads 24-36 Inches Per Year — Contain With Edging

Lysimachia nummularia is the fastest-spreading ground cover. ''Aurea'' (golden foliage) is less aggressive than the green species. Contain with steel landscape edging buried 4-6 inches deep — the runners spread on the soil surface and will not cross a buried barrier. In moist soil, one plant covers a 3-foot circle in one season.

## 9. Dwarf Mondo Grass Looks Like Turf But Never Needs Mowing

Ophiopogon japonicus ''Nana'' grows 2-3 inches tall in dense clumps. From 5 feet away, it reads as lawn. It never needs mowing. Zones 6-10. Shade to partial shade. Slow to establish (3-4 years to full coverage at 8-inch spacing) but virtually indestructible once established.

## 10. Use Landscape Edging to Contain Spreading Ground Covers

Steel edging buried 4-6 inches deep creates an underground barrier that surface-spreading ground covers (creeping Jenny, ajuga, vinca if you ignore Tip #3) cannot cross. Install the edging 2-3 inches above soil level as well — runners can and do climb over flush edging. This is mandatory for any ground cover described as "vigorous" or "fast-spreading" — code words for "will consume your garden if not contained."
